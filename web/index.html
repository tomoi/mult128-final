Web Development

When I build a website, I start by planning out the aspects in Figma (rip Adobe Xd). This is to ensure I have a solid plan before I start so I'm not stuck in the middle wondering what to do next.

Planning for this site

This site was made in a bit of a rush. Since it was a final project at the end of the school year, I had 3 or 4 other projects due around the same time.

This is the very high-quality drawing I made to plan this website. I blocked out how I wanted the content to be displayed, and wrote reminders underneath and around.

I wanted to get the nav bar to look visually appealing so I decided to build it in Figma beforehand. I was trying different things to decide how I wanted the hover and active states to look.
Obviously some changes were made in development.

I brainstorm so I can decide what content goes where beforehand. This helps with clarity and makes sure the pages aren't cluttered.

I use Adobe Color to pick the colours for my webpages. Since this is my personal website, I made the colour scheme my favorite colour, green. I also use Adobe Color to check my colour scheme for accessibility, in terms of contrast and colour blind safety.
